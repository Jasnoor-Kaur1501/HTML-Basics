Extraction Is Not Strength

Strong coffee is not highly extracted coffee. It is highly concentrated coffee.

Extraction measures how much material leaves the bean. Strength measures how much material is in the cup.

You can have strong under-extracted coffee. You can have weak over-extracted coffee.

Confusing these is why most coffee tastes bad.

Coffee flavor is built in layers.

The Control Variables

Every brew method manipulates the same small set of forces.

Grind size controls surface area. Water controls solubility. Time controls sequencing. Agitation controls speed. Ratio controls concentration.

Recipes are just fixed positions inside this system.

The Three Stages of Extraction

Coffee does not extract evenly.

First come acids and volatile aromatics. Then sugars and oils. Then bitter structural compounds.

Great coffee is not strength. It is timing.
