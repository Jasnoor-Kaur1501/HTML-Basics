Methods Are Not Recipes

Brew methods are not instructions. They are systems.

Every method is a way of controlling how water meets coffee. Pressure. Flow. Contact time. Turbulence. Filtration.

Espresso, pour-over, cold brew — these are not drinks. They are extraction environments.

The Two Brewing Families

Almost every brew method belongs to one of two families.

Immersion — coffee sits fully in water. (French press, cupping, cold brew)

Percolation — water flows through coffee. (Pour-over, drip machines, espresso)

Immersion maximizes uniformity. Percolation maximizes control.

Pressure vs Gravity

Some brewers rely on gravity. Others force water through coffee.

Gravity methods are slow, gentle, and clarity-driven. Pressure methods are fast, violent, and texture-driven.

Espresso compresses extraction into seconds. Pour-over stretches it across minutes.

The force you use decides which compounds dominate the cup.

Contact Time Spectrum

Coffee can extract in seconds or in hours.

Espresso: 20–35 seconds Pour-over: 2–4 minutes French press: 4–6 minutes Cold brew: 12–24 hours

Short contact emphasizes acids and aromatics. Long contact emphasizes sugars and heavier compounds.

Time is not convenience. It is chemistry.
