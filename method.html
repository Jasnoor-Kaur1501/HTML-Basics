Brew Methods

Different methods extract different stories from the same bean.

Pour Over

Slow, controlled, clarity-driven.

Espresso

Pressure. Speed. Concentration.

French Press

Immersion for body and oils.

Pour Over

Pour over is gravity-driven extraction. Water passes once through coffee.

It emphasizes clarity, acidity, and separation of flavors.

Pour over rewards grind control, pouring structure, and patience.

Espresso

Espresso is pressurized extraction. Water is forced through coffee at high pressure.

It emphasizes body, oils, dissolved solids, and intensity.

Espresso compresses chemistry into seconds. Small changes create massive shifts.

French Press

French press is full immersion brewing. Coffee steeps entirely in water.

It emphasizes texture, oils, and roundness.

It is forgiving, but reveals grind quality more than technique.

Cold Brew

Cold brew extracts without heat, over long time scales.

It emphasizes sugars, low acidity, and heavy compounds.

Cold brewing changes not just strength, but chemical balance.

AeroPress

AeroPress is a hybrid system. Immersion plus pressure.

It allows control over time, turbulence, and force.

AeroPress is less a method and more a brewing laboratory.

Methods Are Tradeoffs

Every method amplifies something and sacrifices something.

Clarity trades body. Speed trades control. Pressure trades forgiveness.

There is no best method. Only intended outcomes.
